About Me

Precision meets
purpose.

I'm an M.Eng. candidate in Biomedical Engineering at Boston University, working at the intersection of neurotechnology, embedded systems, and device design. My current work focuses on developing smart eyewear for photosensitive epilepsy prevention through real-time physiological signal monitoring.

I came to engineering from biology and behavioral science, which means I never lose sight of the human at the end of the device. I've volunteered in perioperative surgical care, worked as a medical assistant, and built systems from PCB up — all with the same goal: technology that actually helps people.

Epilepsy Prevention Smart Eyewear

Developing voltage-controlled lens eyewear (LCD, LCoS, electrochromic) with integrated PPG monitoring to mitigate photosensitive seizure triggers in real time. Arduino-based prototypes validated attenuation of 3–30 Hz seizure-provoking light stimuli.

03Structural Engineering

7-Joint Optimized Acrylic Truss

Designed and built a 7-joint acrylic truss under strict material constraints using MATLAB structural modeling. Iteratively optimized geometry to improve predicted load capacity from 38 oz to 53.07 oz — final structure held 55.38 oz, accurately predicting the critical buckling member.

04Design Competition · Interactive Installation

Pulsing Anatomical Heart Installation

Interactive lighting installation for a design competition — a 3D-printed translucent PETG anatomical heart pulses in real time with a user's heartbeat via PPG sensing. An internal LED diffuses light to the beat, an LCD displays live BPM, and a speaker mimics hospital heart monitor audio. Electronics housed in a custom 3D-printed enclosure. Light, data, and sound unified into one immersive biomedical experience.
